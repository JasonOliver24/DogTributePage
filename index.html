Here's a timeline of the domestication of dogs

  • 31,000 BCE - Oldest recorded canine fossil, Pleistocene dog from Siberia, Russia
  • 8,000 BCE - Grave in ancient Greece containing the remains of a woman and a puppy
  • 2500 BCE - Ancient Egyptians began domesticating dogs
  • 74 CE - A frieze dating back to the time of the Roman empire in the ruins of Pompeii depicts a dog with a blind man
  • 1250 - A scroll depicts a dog guiding a man through a busy street in China
  • 1561 - Italian sculptor Leone Leoni creates a medal that portrays a dog leading an old blind man with a cane
  • 1780 - The Parisian hospital for the blind, Les Quinze-Vingts, started training dogs for people who were blind
  • 1919 - Germany pioneered the use of dogs to help guide veterans who were blind. By 1927, an estimated 4,000 Germans were using guide
  • 1923 - Dorothy Harrison Eustis started breeding and training German Shepherds in nearby Switzerland to work as police and military dogs
  • 1990 - Ihe Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) is passed, defining the idea that service dogs could be used to help people with disabilities
